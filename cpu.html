central processing unit

The central proscessing unit or CPU takes in all the the data and spits out more data. It consists of cores, the more cores the faster it can be, but the speed of a computer is it's clock speed. The clock keeps all the compants sincronized and it's speed is mesured in giga hertz GHz or mega hertz MHz. In these cores is a ALU arthimatic logic unit which does addition, subtraction, and bit-wise-logic: AND,NOT, and OR. To do multiplacation and division it contains registers which store numbers and a multiplexer or MUX to controll what goes on the final companent; the bus which transfers data to the entire computer.
