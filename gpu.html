graphics processing unit

Despite the name, the GPU doesn't just handle graphics. While the CPU is good at doing things over and over again in a sequence, which is good for reading and running instructions and programs. The GPU is good at doing a lot of things all at once, like drawing 4 million pixels 120 times every second (FPS: frames per second). But it's also good for things like simulations or training AI because you need to do many tasks all at once. You may be wondering why we can't just have the cpu multi task and it's because in order to achieve this GPUs are massive, power hungry, loud, and expensive. For reference a decent CPU is about $260 while a high end GPU is over $2,000! And pulls over 60 watts; 20 volts 3 amps, yikes. But you can't ignore the benefits because even with a low end gpu you can get at least a 40% performance increase!
